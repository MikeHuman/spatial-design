Timeline

1000-1500

The area known as Christchurch belongs to Puari, a settlement of Waitaha people (early iwi). Later this area was used by the NgƒÅi Tahu iwi for mahinga kai (food gathering)


1850 March

The area is surveyed by early settlers Joseph Thomas and Edward Jollie, envisaging Cathedral Square as the city centre


1852 March

First Bridge is built across the Avon River, called the Papanui Bridge or Market Place Bridge


1863 August

Lights installed on Colombo and Papanui bridges for pedestrians


1864 September

Bridge is replaced with a more permanent structure, renamed to the Victoria Bridge


1877 October

Petition to rename Whately Road to Victoria Street passed


1896

Redeveloped as a park


1901 June 22

Foundation stone for a Queen Victoria Statue is unveiled by George V, Duke of Cornwall and York, who was touring New Zealand at the time


1903 January

The Queen Victoria statue arrives from England


1903 May 24

The Queen Victoria statue is unveiled on Empire Day (her birthday)


1904 April 7

Relief panels for the Queen Victoria statue are unveiled by Governor-General Lord Ranfurly


1921

Henry Bowker donated funds to build the Bowker Fountain on a former animal pound


1929

Matthew Frank Burnett donates money for a James Cook statue


1931

Bowker Fountain completed


1932 August 10

Unveiling ceremony for James Cook statue


1953

Floral Clock is presented to citizens of Christchurch


1989

Victoria Square is under redevelopment, reinforcing many instalments and buildings in case of earthquake. The bridge is renamed for the mayor of Christchurch: Sir Hamish Hay


1994

Poupou is built


2004 April 2

Hamish Hay Bridge is registered as a Category II Heritage Site by the NZ Historic Places trust, registration number 1832


2018 April

Time Capsule is buried
