Timeline

1873 May

A bridge to cross the Avon River on Cashel Street was built


1919 July

After months of debate around replacing the bridge and multiple different locations considered, a Bridge was Remembrance was suggested by schoolteacherâ€™s wife L. W. Irwin in a letter to The Press


1920 February

The city council recommended a war memorial committee for Canterbury veterans who fought in World War I. During a meeting, reference to a letter from Reverend W. B. Scott, NZ Expeditionary Force Chaplain, suggested a memorial arch to accommodate the bridge


1920 June

A plan and fundraising for the bridge would go ahead


1921 February

The decision to build the bridge was made, and would replace the bridge that servicemen would use to march from King Edward Barracks on Cashel Street to the railway station when overseeing service


1921

The bridge is designed by William Gummer, partner of Wellington architectural practice Gummer and Prouse


1922 August

Tender for the construction of the bridge is awarded to construction company D. Scott and Son


1923 January 23

Bridge begins construction


1923 April 25

Foundation stone is laid by Governor-General, Admiral of the Fleet, Lord Jellicoe


1924 November 11

Bridge of Remembrance is opened on Armistice Day by Viscount Jellicoe of Scapa


1977 April 25

The Bridge is closed off to vehicular traffic and reopens as a pedestrian precinct


1985 April 2

The Bridge is registered to the New Zealand Historic Places Trust as a Category I Heritage Structure, registration number 289


1989

Stage 1 of refurbishing the Bridge is completed


1991 September

Stage 2 of the Bridge refurbishment commences


1992 April

Stage 2 refurbishments are completed


1992 June 21

Commemorative plaque dedication ceremony is held to celebrate the completion of the refurbishment


2011 February 22

Bridge suffers minor structural damage during the earthquake


2012 January - April

Test are carried out on the bridge to determine the best course of action to undergo repairs from the earthquake


2013 May

Earthquake repairs and strengthening commences


2015 September

Repairs are completed


2016 April 25

The Bridge of Remembrance reopens to the public
