Memorial

Queen Victoria Statue

“Victoria
Born May 24th. 1819,
Died January 22nd. 1901.”

“This stone was laid
by
His Royal Highness
The Duke of Cornwall
& York
June 22nd 1901”

The Duke and Duchess at the time were touring New Zealand on a royal visit, and laid the foundation stone for the Queen Victoria statue. The statue was created by English sculptor Francis John Williamson. The statue itself is bronze cast, and contains relief panels commemorating education, agriculture, pastoralism, manufacture of Canterbury. These panels were unveiled later when they finished, along with a panel listing the Cantabrian soldiers who fought and died in the Second Boer War in South Africa. The inscription reads “In memory of Canterbury’s Sons, South Africa 1900-1902” and lists the names of 89 soldiers. Surrounding the statue are flower beds, each containing immense amounts of Purple tulips (symbolising royalty), and white and red dianthus (symbolising gratitude and admiration).

James Cook Statue

“James Cook
Captain Royal Navy
Circumnavigator
Who first hoisted the
British flag in New Zealand
And explored her seas
And coasts
1769-70 1773-4 1777
Oceani investigator acerrimus”

“Presented by
Matthew Frank Barnett
A citizen of Christchurch
1932”


The statue was unveiled by Governor-General Lord Bledisloe On August 10th 1932. The design was picked from a design competition, the winner being Christchurch sculptor William Trethewey. It is made of carved marble, and based on a plaster model. Surrounding the statue are similar beds of flowers to Queen Victoria, with white and red dianthus. However, there are white tulips instead of purple, representing respect and honour as opposed to royalty.
